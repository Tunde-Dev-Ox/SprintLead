Product Bootcamp Curriculum

Our product bootcamp is for people with prior product management. If this isn't you, check out our recommended courses.

Week 1

Define a Clear Problem

  • Meet your team (Software Engineer(s) & UX Designer)
  • Organize a project kickoff to brainstorm problems
  • Start conducting user research to talk to potential customers

Week 2

Identify Possible Solutions

  • Explore the solution space of your problem and validate which ones will work for your customers
  • Start considering technical feasibility and constraints
  • Start creating user flows, wireframes and user journeys for your proposed solution

Week 3

Prioritize Features and Create Low-Fidelity Designs

  • Align on the core features your team plans to deliver to customers
  • Start designing initial visual concepts and mockups
  • Break down your user stories into executable technical tasks

Week 4

Development Kickoff & Technical Prototype

  • Explain and establish your team's chosen Agile methodology (e.g., Scrum, Kanban)
  • Prioritize user stories and estimate development effort for the first sprint.
  • Engineers begin building a basic, functional prototype to validate core functionalities.
  • Refine initial design concepts based on user feedback and technical constraints. Conduct usability testing on the prototype with real users.

Week 5

Market Validation & Competitive Analysis

  • Analyze your direct and indirect competitors, identifying their strengths and weaknesses.
  • Research your target market size, growth projections, and key trends.
  • Define your unique value proposition and how you stand out from the competition.
  • Explore potential revenue models and finalize your monetization strategy.

Week 6

MVP Development & User Onboarding

  • Focus on the features necessary for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) launch.
  • Engineers refine the prototype, fix bugs, and prepare for MVP launch.
  • Design a seamless user onboarding experience for first-time users.
  • Initiate marketing activities to generate buzz and pre-launch awareness.

Week 7

Launch & Data-Driven Iteration

  • Deploy the MVP on your chosen platform and activate user acquisition channels.
  • Set up analytics dashboards to track key metrics and user behavior.
  • Run A/B tests on critical features to identify areas for improvement.
  • Collect user feedback and iterate on the product roadmap.

Week 8

Growth Hacking & User Engagement

  • Implement low-cost, creative tactics to rapidly acquire and retain users.
  • Foster user engagement through forums, social media, and customer support.
  • Create valuable content to educate and attract potential users.
  • Analyze user churn data and implement strategies to keep users engaged.

Week 9

Funding & Fundraising Preparation

  • Craft a compelling pitch deck to present your product to potential investors.
  • Update your financial projections and refine your business model based on data insights.
  • Practice delivering investor pitches and connect with potential partners.
  • Prepare for investor negotiations and understand key terms in term sheets.

Week 10

Demo Day & Graduation

  • Teams present their completed products to a panel of judges, investors, and mentors.
  • Receive valuable feedback and celebrate achievements with graduation ceremony and awards.
  • Join the Sprintleaps alumni network for ongoing support and collaboration.
  • Leverage career resources and mentorship to secure your next product management role.
